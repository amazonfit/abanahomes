How often should I water the bonsai plants purchased online?

Watering your bonsai tree is important. You should know the watering rules.

Thumb Rule of Watering Bonsai Plants
Touch the soil and if you feel the soil as dry, it’s the time you need to water your bonsai plants. Water it fully until the water start flowing from the hole below the pot.

Indoor Bonsai Plants Watering Schedule
You should water your indoor bonsai plants every alternate day in summer and twice a week in winters. This will keep your bonsai plants hydrated enough to grow well.

Outdoor Bonsai Plants Watering Schedule
Outdoor Bonsai Plants goes dry faster than the indoor bonsai plants. Hence, you should water it daily in the summer and every alternate day in winters. Stick to this schedule and do check the soil before watering the bonsai plant.

Carmona Bonsai Plant

This bonsai has small oval leaves and gives a tradition feeling. Delicate white blossom bloom almost year round and small dark green leaves are shiny with little white dots on the surfaces. The trunk has wonderful brownish red bark which contrasts beautifully with the dark green of its leaves.

Chinese Elm Bonsai Plant

The Chinese Elm has beautiful, distinctive dark grey to reddish brown bark that turns fissure and corky when mature. It has a lovely branching shape with delicate, oval shaped toothed leaves that showcase a fresh green color and a delightfully tiny leaf. It has a highly predictable growth pattern, making it ideal for beginners who are just getting used to training Bonsai.

Ficus S-shaped Bonsai Plant

This bonsai tree has an s-shaped stem, which gives a dramatic look. Most ficus Bonsai trees can produce aerial roots in their natural habitat, which are often presented in appealing Bonsai creations.
